# Decision Example

## Visible output

Choose the path with the fastest paid signal, not the path that feels most complete. A polished plan without a buyer signal is just a nicer delay.

The move is to sell one narrow result to one narrow buyer, then let payment decide whether the larger idea deserves oxygen.

## Plan

Run a seven-day paid-signal test.

- Actor: the person who can reach the first buyer directly.
- Input: one buyer type, one painful problem, one small paid outcome.
- Action: make a direct offer to 10 qualified buyers before building the full product.
- Output: deposits, objections, delivery time, and buyer language.
- Pass condition: 3 deposits or one buyer willing to pay the target price within 7 days.
- Kill condition: 10 direct offers produce no serious price conversation.

## Author check only, not user output

- Evidence: interest is not validation; payment or serious price conversation is.
- Friction: narrow offer lowers trust cost.
- Boundary: do not build landing pages, decks, or automations before the paid-signal test.
