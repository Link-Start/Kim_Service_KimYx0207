# Path

## Structure

```text
Subject -> Motive -> Interpretation -> Action -> Resistance -> Signal -> State Change -> Continuation
```

## Subject

Who experiences the result?

Good subject:

```text
the person who must decide
the receiver of the draft
the operator who runs the workflow
the user who enters the product
```

## Motive

Why does the subject enter now?

Look for:

- pain
- desire
- pressure
- curiosity
- obligation
- risk
- expected gain

## Interpretation

What does the subject understand first?

Check:

- Does the subject know what this is?
- Does the subject know why it matters?
- Does the subject know what to do next?

## Action

What is the first meaningful action?

A first action must be small, visible, and testable.

## Resistance

What blocks movement?

Common resistance:

- unclear promise
- too many choices
- weak trust
- high effort
- missing proof
- fear of loss
- delayed feedback

## Signal

What feedback appears after action?

A signal can be a reply, click, payment, error, test result, usage event, saved draft, or changed state.

## State Change

What changes after the signal?

Examples:

- unknown becomes known
- doubt becomes trust
- vague idea becomes decision
- blocked workflow becomes running workflow

## Continuation

What happens next?

Examples:

- scale
- stop
- revise
- buy
- share
- repeat
- verify
- hand off
